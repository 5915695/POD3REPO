Domestic Products

Russet Potato

Russet are medium/large in size and are long, cylindrical, and oblong in shape with a slightly flattened appearance. There are a few shallow eyes scattered across the surface. The flesh is pale white, firm, dense, and floury. Cooked Russet potatoes become fluffy and light with a smooth, buttery flavor and mild, earthy undertones.

Yukon Gold

Yukon Gold potatoes are slightly flat and oval in shape with light gold, thin skin, and light yellow flesh. They can be identified by the rosy pink coloration of the shallow eyes.The skin is also speckled with many small, brown spots.Yukon Gold potatoes take on a creamy and tender consistency with a rich, buttery, and earthy flavor.

Jewel Yam

A member of the Convolvulaceae family along with other herbaceous perennial vines, trees and shrubs. Jewel yams are cylindrical in shape, with tapering ends. Jewel Yams have a rough semi-thick skin with a reddish/pink tinged pale orange color. The interior is brilliant orange. The flesh of the Jewel Yam is moist and starchy.

Russian Banana

Russian banana are a member of the nightshade or Solanaceae family, along with eggplants, tobacco, and petunias. They are slender, delicious potatoes with a wide range of culinary applications. They belong to the “fingerling” group of potatoes and the tuber’s slight curve and golden color earned the potato it's fruity epithet.

Japanese Sweet

Japanese Sweets, also referred to as an Oriental, Kotobuki, Satsuma Imo, or Satsumaimo, are low moisture potatoes with deep red outer skin. It is an excellent choice for roasting, steaming or stir-frying because they can be cooked more effectively than other potato varieties that do not cook as well using faster cooking methods.

French Fingerling

Rose-pink skin and creamy yellow flesh splashed with pink make French Fingerlings as beautiful as it is delicious. Larger and thicker than other fingerling types, with an outstanding nutty flavor—this variety is perfect for any preparation. Try them baked, boiled, fried, air-fried, mashed, in salad or for cooking—you can't miss.

Yuca

Yuca roots are similar in size and shape to sweet potatoes and can be anywhere from one to several pounds in size, growing up to 4 feet below the ground. Their starchy flesh is a light white or cream color with a grainy texture similar to potatoes. The meaty flesh is described as having a mild, sweet, somewhat nutty taste.

All Blue

A wonderfully flavorful and colorful potato with meaty flesh that makes a great mashed potato. Skin is rich purple. Inside it's a blue that turns pale when cooked. With deep blue skin and blue flesh sporting a white ring, tubers are oblong with deep eyes. Excellent flavor cooked in any way – sautéed, steamed, or mashed.
